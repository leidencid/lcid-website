NVP 2022 talks and posters

By  Lara M. Wierenga, PhD Lina van Drunen, MSc Mara van der Meulen, PhD Michelle Achterberg, PhD Simone Dobbelaar, MSc

It was so wonderful to meet in person again at NVP 2022 in Egmond aan Zee April 28-30. Simone Dobbelaar presented her work on “Retaliatory responses in childhood: an fMRI test-replication design”.


Furthermore we share our posters here:

Lina van Drunen presented her poster on “Music alters brain development: A longitudinal twin study on sensorimotor synchronization and brain plasticity”

Mara van der Meulen talked about “Growing up prosocially? Longitudinal trajectoreis of prosocial behavior from middle childhood into adolescence”

Michelle Achterberg presented a poster on “Longitudinal associations between social media use, mental well-being and structural brain development across adolescence”

Lara Wierenga showed the preregistration and preliminary results of her project “Understanding vulnerability through variability: a longitudinal study on sex differences in brain development in relation to psychiatric conditions using normative modeling”
